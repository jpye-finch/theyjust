# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Parents across the US, UK, and Europe with children aged 0–5. They are tired, often holding a baby,
using the app one-handed in stolen moments: a nursery at 6am, a waiting room,
the sofa after bedtime. Two jobs: capture a first before the memory blurs
("They just rolled over!"), and get a calm answer to "is this normal?" without
being ranked or alarmed. Co-parents share one family; grandparents arrive later.
Emotional stakes are high — this app holds their child's story and touches their
deepest anxieties. Premature babies' families are first-class users (corrected age).

## Product Purpose

TheyJust is a milestone memory app: a timeline of firsts (photo, note, who was
there) fused with clinically sourced typical-age ranges (WHO / CDC / NHS, every
range cross-checked, citations stored). It succeeds when a parent opens it at a
milestone moment by reflex, and closes it feeling reassured, never judged.
Positioning: the milestone memory app that also reassures — against clinical
screeners (CDC app), exhausting daily trackers (Huckleberry), and context-free
photo journals (Tinybeans).

## Brand Personality

Tender, unhurried, quietly joyful. The feel of a hand-written baby book, not a
dashboard. Celebration is warm and personal, never loud, never gamified. The
product's voice is already shipped and reviewed: verb phrases composed into
"They just …!", ranges phrased as "typically emerges between…", a gentle
signpost that points to the child's doctor. Warm plain English that reads
naturally in the US, UK, and Europe — no market-specific jargon. The visual
identity must feel like the same person wrote both.

## Anti-references

- The stock AI "warm parenting app" kit: sage green + warm cream + soft
  terracotta, Bricolage Grotesque, identical white rounded cards, 999px pills,
  tracked uppercase labels. The current Figma file is the anti-reference.
- CDC Milestone Tracker's clinical sterility; checklist-as-exam energy.
- Tracker density (Huckleberry, Baby Tracker): grids of stats, streaks, charts.
- Pastel-nursery cliché: clouds, stars, mint-and-baby-blue, infantilised icons.
- Anything that ranks, scores, percentiles, or implies a deadline.
- Market-locked clinical jargon in either direction ("pediatrician",
  "health visitor" alone) — signposts say "your child's doctor or health
  visitor", which reads everywhere.

## Design Principles

- **The words are the heroes.** "They just took their first steps!" is the
  product's best asset; typography carries the celebration, decoration doesn't.
- **Never a deadline.** No red states, no "behind", no progress percentages
  against age. Ranges reassure; achieved milestones celebrate regardless of timing.
- **One-handed and interruptible.** Capture must survive a wriggling baby:
  huge targets, instant save, nothing lost on interruption.
- **Credibility worn lightly.** Citations and sourced ranges are present but
  quiet — a footnote a parent can trust, not a banner shouting "clinical".
- **Chosen, not generated.** Every visual decision should feel like a person
  with taste made it; if a choice is guessable from the category name, replace it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout. Respect dynamic type / font scaling (parents use
large text at 3am). Honour reduced-motion. Form errors use accessible live
regions (already the shipped pattern). One-handed reach: primary actions in the
bottom half of the screen. Photography and copy inclusive of all family shapes;
premature-baby corrected age is a first-class, non-othering experience.
